Dish of the Week

Chicken Parm Pasta

Difficulty: Intermediate | Prep Time: 30 min | Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • Kosher salt
  • 12 oz. store-bought cooked frozen breaded chicken cutlets
  • 1 lb. cavatappi or other short pasta
  • 1 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 4 cups store-bought or homemade marinara sauce
  • 2 oz. Parmesan, finely grated (about 1 cup), divided, plus more for serving
  • 3/4 cup shredded mozzarella
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Torn fresh basil, for serving

Instructions

  1. Cook frozen chicken according to package instructions. Let cool, then cut crosswise into strips.
  2. Arrange a rack in center of oven; preheat to 400°. In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook pasta, stirring occasionally, until al dente according to package directions. Drain.
  3. Meanwhile, in a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat, heat oil. Cook onion, stirring occasionally, until translucent, about 7 minutes. Add garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, around 1 minute more. Add marinara and 1 tsp. salt and bring to a low simmer.
  4. Add pasta to skillet and stir to coat in sauce. Add 1/2 cup Parmesan and toss until melted. Top with 1/2 cup Parmesan, then sprinkle with mozzarella. Arrange chicken on top of mozzarella.
  5. Bake chicken Parm pasta until cheese is melted and chicken is warmed through, 10 to 12 minutes.
  6. Season with a few grinds of pepper. Top with basil and more Parmesan.
